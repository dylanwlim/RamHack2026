How it works

Three quick steps from prescription details to a likely fill.

  1. 01

    Capture the exact prescription

    Search by medication, then narrow by dose and formulation so the lookup reflects the real fill request, not a generic substitute.

  2. 02

    Compare nearby pharmacy snapshots

    Surface neighborhood, distance, stock state, and last refresh in one clean list instead of forcing patients to call locations one at a time.

  3. 03

    Route the patient to the best next move

    Highlight likely full fills first, keep partial fills visible, and make the availability story obvious enough for a quick demo or real triage conversation.

Why it matters

Medication access fails in the details.

Shortages rarely break at the brand level. They break at the exact dosage, release type, and nearby inventory level patients actually need.

Less phone-tree chaos

Patients and caregivers should not have to bounce across five pharmacies to learn that the XR version is out while the standard tablet is available.

Better handoffs for care teams

Nurses, pharmacists, and office staff need a fast answer they can relay with confidence while the patient is still on the line.

Demo-friendly clarity

In a hackathon pitch, judges can understand the pain and the product value in one screen: one prescription, several locations, clearly different outcomes.
