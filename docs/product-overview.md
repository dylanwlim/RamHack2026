# Product Overview

PharmaPath is a medication-access navigation product. Its job is to help people move from uncertainty to a better next step: a better first pharmacy call, a clearer backup plan, or a more informed prescribing decision.

## Core Surfaces

- `Pharmacy Finder`: Pairs a medication with a location and returns a nearby shortlist designed for action.
- `Medication Lookup`: Focuses on medication-wide access context, not store-level availability.
- `Methodology`: Explains what the product can support directly, what is inferred, and where the boundary stays firm.

## What Users Get

- A clearer first-call path instead of a random nearby list.
- Medication context that can explain why a fill may be easy, mixed, or higher-friction.
- Better questions to ask a pharmacy before sending someone to pick up a prescription.
- A separate contributor layer that can add context without being confused with confirmed inventory.

## Typical Workflows

- A patient searches a medication and neighborhood to decide which pharmacy to call first.
- A caregiver checks whether a refill might require extra lead time before making a trip.
- A prescriber reviews access friction and backup options before routing a prescription.
- A signed-in contributor adds a report that may help future users judge whether a pharmacy is worth trying.

## Product Boundaries

- PharmaPath can help with navigation, prioritization, and question quality.
- PharmaPath does not claim that a medication is currently on the shelf at a specific store.
- PharmaPath does not promise same-day pickup, insurance approval, or clinical suitability.

## Responsible Use

- Call the pharmacy directly before assuming availability.
- Use clinician judgment for treatment decisions and alternatives.
- Treat the product as guidance for workflow and communication, not as a substitute for care.
